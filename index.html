Automation

This is the process by which data is digitised and converted into information or becomes an actual service using various programming languages and algorithms. The four types of automation are—Basic Automation, Process Automation, Integration Automation, and Artificial Intelligence (AI).

Process Automation

This type of automation makes use of software apps that would handle business processes for transparency and uniformity. Examples of Process Automation are:

  • Kissflow
  • IBM Maximo
  • Happay
  • Workday
  • Zalamea
  • Nisoft
  • SAP

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI are digital machines that are capable of storing electromechanically sensed data for machine learning and making decisions or actions based on this information. Machine Learning is a subset of AI which allows machines to learn from past data and continuously improve knowledge based on appended relevant information. AI can be found in:

  • Automated financial investing
  • Manufacturing robots
  • Self-driving cars
  • Disease mapping
  • Proactive healthcare management
  • Social media monitoring
  • Virtual travel booking agent

Basic Automation

This type of automation takes simple, essential tasks and automates them. A high level of digitisation is used as a tool to centralise repetitive tasks. Examples of Basic Automation technologies are:

  • IBM Cloud Pack for Automation
  • AWS
  • Google Cloud
  • Digital Ocean
  • Rackspace

Integration Automation

In this type of automation, systems are programmed to perform specific tasks to work along with humans as part of the team. Complex cases that cannot be handled by digital workers are passed to human counterparts while simpler tasks that can be verified through SMS, chat or email can be automatically done by the digital workers. Examples of Integration Automation are:

  • IBM Ocash - automates the verification of invoice accuracy
  • Digital retailers to check on stock quantities and pricing
  • Customer interface answering machines and chatbots
  • Employee data collection

Cyber Security

This technology protects businesses from outside threats to their digital information. Examples of cyber security are:

  • Blockchain - a system of logging information across different digital storage locations within the network that makes it impossible or difficult to trace or hack
  • VPN - securing business digital communication through encrypted data tunnels
  • Firewall - software that prohibits system access from an unauthorised source

Operational Assessment

We examine existing processes of the business starting from the acquisition of customer needs, development, and transformation of various inputs to realise and satisfy these needs, analyse the processes, and identify ones that are redundant, ineffective, and inefficient.

Operational Streamlining

We formulate new and improved processes that spend less time and resources but produce the same output. We also track and log the test runs of new processes, document results, and present to the client the final output of the modified and improved processes and procedures.

Business Risk Analysis

We scan the internal and external business environment for potential risks. These include internal risks, such as:

  • Financial instability
  • Technology issues
  • Strategic management errors
  • System hazards

Our analyses also consider external risks, such as:

  • Political instability
  • Legal liabilities
  • Power interruption
  • SysCompetitors
  • Natural disasters

We also identify and define the cost and overall impact on processes, resources, and products/services of these risks, and document and present findings of the business risk study to the client.
Risk Management Strategies

Our risk management strategies range from the identification of human resource training needs on risk mitigation techniques, facilitating the conduct of training either from in-house or by contracted experts, developing control plans, projects, and measures to mitigate risks as well as developing disaster preparedness protocols

The risk management tools that Zenith Management will introduce are:

  • HIRAC
  • Safe Work Permitting
  • Safety Onboarding
  • Legal Compliance Team
  • Property Insurance Availment
  • Toolbox/Pre-task Meeting
  • Disaster Preparedness Exercises
  • Internal Readiness Audits
  • Management Review

Project Identification

We define what the project is all about, why it needs to be done, and the scope of activities required to complete it in order to develop clear, actionable development strategies and milestones.

Task Delegation and Oversight

We identify persons who are responsible for specific tasks, communicate and delegate accordingly, calendar activities, and set clear deadlines. We also perform oversight when the activities are executed in order to ensure the progress remains smooth, consistent, and free of any challenges or obstacles.

Progress Tracking

We make use of tracking tools to monitor the progress of scheduled activities as well as the cost incurred per task and provide a detailed summary. We use a variety of tools suited to diverse project and business needs, including:

  • ProofHub
  • Scoro
  • Workzone
  • Filestage
  • Proprofs
  • Zoho Projects
  • GanttPRO
KPIs are monitored and communicated to all stakeholders using the following general criteria:
  • Timeliness - to make sure tasks are completed on time according to the agreed schedule
  • Budget - to keep track of whether or not the cost expended on each task is over, under or within budget
  • Quality - to gauge satisfaction among those who are working and benefitting from the project
  • Effectiveness - to make sure the results of tasks fall within or exceed what was planned

Zenith Management will meet regularly with stakeholders and discuss milestones, issues and constraints met during the execution of activities, find solutions, resolve issues, and convey all relevant information to your in-house management team.
